Color Science

Wright–Guild Color-Matching Experiment

Introduction to the Wright and Guild RGB color-matching experiments.

Purpose of the Experiments

Observers adjusted three primary lights until their mixture visually matched a monochromatic test light.

Color-Matching Coefficients

The required amounts of red, green, and blue primaries became the RGB color-matching coefficients.

Negative Coefficients

For some wavelengths, one primary had to be added to the test side. This produced a negative coefficient in the mathematical representation.
