Color Science

Human Vision

Learn how the human eye and visual system produce color perception.

Introduction

Human vision begins when light enters the eye and is focused onto the retina. The retina contains photoreceptors that convert light into electrical signals.

Rods and Cones

Rods are highly sensitive to low light but do not provide normal color vision. Cones support color vision and are commonly grouped into S-, M-, and L-cone classes.

Why This Matters

Colorimetry is based on human visual responses. Understanding the eye helps explain why color can be represented using three primary quantities.
